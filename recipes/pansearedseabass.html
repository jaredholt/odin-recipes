Pan Seared Chilean Sea Bass

I Start Drooling Thinking About This

What is Chilean Sea Bass

Chilean sea bass is actually a nickname for the Patagonian Toothfish. This species of fish becomes oil rich in the cold waters of Antarctica which makes it a delicious fish to cook and eat. It has a mild flavor, is full of rich fatty acids, and is very easy to cook. One interesting fact is that while it is a white fish similar to bass, it's not actually sea bass at all! It can be quite pricey, but it's absolutely worth the splurge!

Ingredients

  • 2 6 Oz Chilean Sea Bass Fillets
  • 2 Tbsp Avocado Oil
  • Salt and Pepper, To Taste
  • 2 Tbsp Ghee or Butter
  • Juice From 1/2 Lemon
  • 2 Tbsp Capers

Instructions

  1. Pat your sea bass fillets dry and coat on both sides with 1 tbsp avocado oil. Sprinkle the fillets with salt and pepper.
  2. Preheat your oven to 425. Heat the remaining 1 tbsp avocado oil in a cast iron (or other oven-safe skillet) over medium high heat. When the pan is nice and hot, add the fillets skin side down (if they have skin). Let them cook, untouched, for 5 mins. Flip and cook for an additional 5 minutes
  3. Transfer the skillet to the oven and cook for an additional 5 minutes. The fish should flake easily with a fork at this point.
  4. Melt the ghee (or butter) for the lemon butter sauce the microwave or in a sauce pan on the stove. Stir in the lemon juice and capers. spoon some of the lemon butter caper sauce over top. Serve and enjoy!
